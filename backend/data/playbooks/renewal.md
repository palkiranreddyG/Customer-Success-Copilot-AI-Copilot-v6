# Playbook: Contract Renewal and Churn Prevention

## Section 1: Identifying Churn Risk
Preventing customer churn is our top priority. A customer is flagged as a high churn risk if their overall health score drops below 50. CSMs must monitor weekly health scores, looking for signs of low usage frequency, unresolved high-priority support tickets, or recent executive departures. If a customer displays multiple churn risk signals, immediately trigger the Escalation Playbook.

## Section 2: Renewal Timeline and Outreach
The renewal process begins 90 days before the contract end date. 
At Day 90: CSM conducts a pre-renewal assessment, calculating the customer's average health score over the last quarter.
At Day 60: Initiate formal outreach to the executive sponsor. Review achievements and ROI.
At Day 30: Present the renewal proposal. If the health score is above 80, aim for an upsell.
At Day 15: Finalize signatures.

## Section 3: Mitigation Strategies for At-Risk Renewals
If a customer is identified as a churn risk during renewal preparation (health score below 60), the CSM must execute mitigation steps. Arrange a business review with decision-makers to address pain points. Offer temporary contract concessions or custom training. Work closely with product teams to fast-track critical feature requests.

## Section 4: Post-Renewal Follow-up
After securing a renewal, thank the client and update their status in the database. Schedule a planning session for the new contract period. Ensure their health score tracking configuration is updated, reflecting any new usage metrics or integration changes.
